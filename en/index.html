About Me

With nearly 15 years of experience in psychology research and study, and a strong belief in the person-centered approach, I see myself as a facilitator dedicated to helping individuals uncover their inner strength and evolve into a more empowered self.

I work with young adults, supporting them on issues revolving identity formation, career uncertainty, and personal growth. I also work with parents,to provide gudiance in how to deal with challenges in parenting and dealing with kids with depression.
I have supported adults who come to me dealing with "midlife crises", major depression, and intimate relationship issues.
I also have worked for the Ministry of Social and Family Development (MSF) to evaluated SESV services dedicated for divorce families at various Family Service Centres (FSCs).

At Yu Xin, we aim to set a safe space where you can express yourself freely and without judgment.

Educational / Training Background:
- Ph.D. in Psychology, Nanyang Technology University, Singapore
- BA(Hons) in Psychology, Nanyang Technology University, Singapore

Don't ignore the burdens on your mind

The body and mind are connected. Often, when our body is unwell, we choose to seek medical help immediately. However, when our thoughts are troubled and we can’t resolve our inner conflicts, we often think we should just endure it, tolerate it, or ignore our own feelings. It seems that listening to our inner self and seeking help for our mental well-being is seen as inappropriate, unnecessary, or a sign of weakness.

In reality, you don’t need to be "sick" to seek help. When you have emotional needs, it doesn’t necessarily mean something is wrong with you, but rather that you want to improve and become happier—a cry for a better self.

Start your journey of self-awareness and enhance your mental health with Yu Xin now.
